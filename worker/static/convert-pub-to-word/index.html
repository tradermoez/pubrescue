Can You Convert a Publisher File to Word? The Honest Answer

Updated June 2026

With Publisher retiring in October 2026, "how do I convert my .pub files to Word?" is the most natural question in the world — Word is the program you'll still have. Here's the answer nobody selling a one-click converter wants to give you: a faithful direct .pub → .docx conversion doesn't exist, and understanding why will save you hours of frustration.

Why direct conversion fails

Publisher and Word model documents in fundamentally different ways:

  • Publisher is a layout program. A bulletin is dozens of text boxes, images, and shapes pinned to exact positions on a page — like a corkboard.
  • Word is a flowing-text program. Content streams from top-left to bottom-right, wrapping and reflowing — like a scroll.

Converting a corkboard into a scroll means either nailing everything in place with anchored text boxes (which produces a Word file that fights you on every edit) or pouring all the text out in reading order (which destroys the design). Every tool that claims direct .pub→Word conversion does one of those two things. Even Microsoft never built this feature into Word — and they owned both formats.

The workflows that actually work

If you need the document to print/share: convert to PDF

This is Microsoft's own recommendation for preserving Publisher files. PubRescue batch-converts .pub files to PDF with a free preview of every page before you pay. Modern Word can also open PDFs and attempt conversion to an editable document (File → Open → select the PDF) — useful for simple documents, and you can judge the PDF quality in the preview first.

If you need to keep editing the design: convert to ODG

ODG (OpenDocument Graphics) preserves the layout model. It opens in LibreOffice Draw — free, actively maintained, Windows/Mac/Linux — and editing it feels close to editing in Publisher. Every PubRescue batch includes ODG alongside PDF, PNG, and SVG.

If you genuinely need Word: rebuild with rescued assets

The realistic version of "convert to Word": get a PDF + PNGs of the original (so you have every image and the exact layout to reference), then recreate the document in Word or Designer using its templates. For documents you produce monthly — bulletins, newsletters — this one-time rebuild into a Word template is usually the right long-term move anyway, since the .pub format is now a dead end.

What about the sites that claim .pub → .docx?

Test them with one important file before trusting them with a folder: you'll typically get a .docx where every paragraph is a floating text box, images sit on top of text, and changing one sentence dislodges the whole page. That's not a conversion bug — it's the corkboard-into-scroll problem, and no amount of "AI-powered" marketing changes the geometry. The honest paths are PDF (perfect for reading/printing), ODG (real editing), or a deliberate rebuild.
