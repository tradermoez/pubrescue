The two traps with "100% free" .pub converters

Trap 1: quality you can't check until it's too late

Most free converters run your file through LibreOffice on a server and hand back a PDF sight-unseen. When fonts get substituted or a complex layout shifts, you find out after you've already uploaded — and if you were converting a stack of files, you've no idea which ones came out wrong. A preview-first tool removes that gamble.

Trap 2: where your file actually goes

"Free" file-conversion sites pay their bills somehow. Read what you're agreeing to before uploading anything with names, addresses, giving records, or member information — a church directory or a client invoice is not something to drop on an anonymous server with vague terms. PubRescue processes files privately and deletes them within 24 hours.

The genuinely-free, no-catch option (if you're technical)

LibreOffice is free, open-source, and installs on Windows, Mac, and Linux. Open a .pub file in its Draw component, then export to PDF. Caveats: fonts you don't have get substituted, very old or very new Publisher files can open imperfectly, and there's no batch mode without learning the command line. If you'll convert files regularly and don't mind a 350MB install, it's a solid free choice. PubRescue exists for everyone who'd rather not.

Why convert now rather than later

Microsoft Publisher retires on October 1, 2026. After that, Microsoft 365 stops opening .pub files entirely, and the free tools that rely on Publisher-format support won't improve — the format is frozen.
