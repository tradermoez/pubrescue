What to Use Instead of Microsoft Publisher (2026)

Updated June 2026 · Publisher retires October 1, 2026

Two separate problems get mixed together when Publisher retires: what do you do with your old files, and what do you make new documents with? This guide covers the second — with the first answered in one line: convert your .pub archive to PDF/ODG before October, because none of the tools below open .pub files well, and most don't open them at all.

LibreOffice Draw — the free, offline workhorse

Best for: ex-Publisher users who want free, installed, no-subscription software — and the only tool here that directly continues editing your converted documents (PubRescue outputs ODG, Draw's native format family).

  • Same corkboard-style editing model as Publisher: text boxes, images, and shapes placed freely on a page.
  • Windows, Mac, Linux. No account, no subscription, no cloud requirement.
  • Honest downsides: a dated interface, and templates are sparse compared to Canva — you'll bring your own designs (which, if you convert your old files to ODG, you already have).

Canva — the easiest path for non-designers

Best for: flyers, social posts, and newsletters when you'd rather start from a polished template than a blank page.

  • Browser-based with a huge free tier and thousands of templates; the learning curve Publisher users feared simply isn't there.
  • Downsides: it's cloud software — you don't own the app, designs live in their format, and some assets push the paid tier. Fine for new work; not an archive strategy.

Affinity Publisher — the professional's replacement

Best for: people who used Publisher seriously and want real desktop publishing — master pages, bleeds, print-grade output — without Adobe's subscription. One-time purchase (~$70), Windows/Mac. It does not open .pub files; bring PDFs or rebuild.

Word, PowerPoint & Designer — Microsoft's official answer

Microsoft's migration guidance points Publisher users at this trio. Truthfully: Word handles text-first documents (letters, simple newsletters) well via templates; PowerPoint is secretly a competent poster/flyer tool (one slide = one page, free placement like Publisher); Designer makes quick AI-generated social graphics. None of them is a real DTP app, and none opens your old files — see why .pub→Word conversion is a trap.
