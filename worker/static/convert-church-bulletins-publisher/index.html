Your Church Bulletins Are Trapped in Publisher. Here's How to Free Them.

Updated June 2026 · For church secretaries, treasurers & volunteers

If your church has made its weekly bulletin in Microsoft Publisher for the last decade or two, this affects you directly: Microsoft is retiring Publisher on October 1, 2026. After that date, the Microsoft 365 version won't even open .pub files. Every bulletin, newsletter, funeral program, and event flyer in that format becomes a file nobody can read.

This guide is written for the person who actually makes the bulletin — usually a church secretary or a faithful volunteer, not an IT professional. No jargon.

Why this is happening (the 30-second version)

Publisher is 35 years old and Microsoft is moving on. They recommend converting your files to PDF and using Word, PowerPoint, or Canva for new documents. The problem: their built-in "Save as PDF" does one file at a time, and most churches have hundreds.

What to rescue first

  • The current year's bulletins and templates — you reuse these every week. Losing the master template means rebuilding from scratch.
  • Anniversary and historical documents — directories, dedication programs, anniversary booklets. These are irreplaceable church history.
  • Recurring designs — offering envelopes, welcome cards, event flyers you re-run yearly.

The plan, step by step

Step 1 — Gather your .pub files in one folder

Search your church computer for *.pub (in Windows File Explorer, type *.pub in the search box). Copy them all into one folder so nothing gets missed. Check the old computer in the office closet too — that's where the archive usually lives.

Step 2 — Convert them to PDF (for keeping) and ODG (for editing)

You have two routes:

  • If Publisher still works on your computer: open each file, File → Export → Create PDF/XPS. Perfect quality, but one file at a time — fine for a dozen, painful for hundreds.
  • For the whole folder at once: PubRescue lets you drag in up to 50 files at a time and converts them together. It shows you a free preview of every page first, so you can see exactly how each bulletin will look before paying anything. You get PDF (for the archive and reprinting) plus ODG (which opens in the free LibreOffice program if you ever need to edit an old design).

Step 3 — Save the converted files in two places

Church computers fail and volunteers change. Keep one copy on the office computer and one in the cloud (the church Google account, Dropbox, or even emailed to yourself). PDFs are universal — any computer, forever.
